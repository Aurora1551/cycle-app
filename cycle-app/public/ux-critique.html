Section 1 — Drop-off Risk Points

Where in the user journey are we most likely to lose someone? Each of these is a moment where a user might close the tab and never come back.

1.5 Content loading failures are cold and unhelpful  🟡 Important
When API content fails to load (network issues, server error), the user sees either a spinner that never resolves or a generic error. For a user who opens the app seeking comfort during a difficult fertility moment, a blank screen or error is the opposite of what they need. This is especially bad on Day 1 when first impressions matter most.

Recommendation
Bundle 5-10 offline fallback affirmations and quotes into the app. When content fails to load, show a warm fallback with a cached quote, auto-retry silently in the background. Never show "Something went wrong" to a user in a vulnerable emotional state. The error should feel like a gentle pause, not a failure.
Section 2 — Missing Features for Fertility Audience

Features that users going through IVF, egg freezing, or other fertility treatments would genuinely benefit from. Prioritised by emotional impact.

2.1 Partner / support person involvement  🟡 Important
Fertility treatment is rarely a solo experience. Partners, best friends, mothers, and support people often feel helpless — they want to help but don't know how. Currently the app is entirely single-player. There's a gift flow but no way for a support person to be part of the daily journey. This is a missed opportunity for connection and for organic growth (support person → new user).
Concept — daily support card
FROM SARAH'S CYCLE
Day 8 — Stimulation Phase
HOW SHE MIGHT BE FEELING
Bloated, tired, anxious about egg count. Injections may be getting harder.
SOMETHING YOU CAN DO
"Run her a bath tonight. Don't ask about the appointment — let her bring it up."
Send her a note 💛
Recommendation
Build a "Support Person" link in Settings. The primary user invites someone via SMS/email. The support person gets a daily card (no app required, just a web link) with: (1) what phase/day the user is on, (2) how they might be feeling, (3) one concrete suggestion for support. This is emotionally powerful, low-effort to build (daily email/SMS with a web card), and a natural growth channel.
2.2 Symptom and side-effect tracking  🟡 Important
IVF and egg freezing come with significant physical side effects: bloating, injection site bruising, mood swings, headaches, nausea, fatigue. Users currently have nowhere to log these. The journal prompt is emotional, not physical. Having a quick daily symptom log would help users track patterns, share with their clinic, and feel validated that what they're experiencing is normal.

Recommendation
Add an optional "Body check-in" card to the daily flow — quick tap on symptoms (no typing needed), optional intensity slider. Show a simple timeline/trend in the Journey tab so users can see patterns. Offer a "Share with my clinic" export. Keep it optional — some days you just want the affirmation and don't want to think about symptoms.
2.3 Two-week wait (TWW) specific support  🔴 Critical
The two-week wait (after embryo transfer, before pregnancy test) is widely described as the hardest part of IVF. The anxiety is extreme. Users are symptom-spotting obsessively, googling constantly, and the uncertainty is unbearable. This is the moment your app should shine — but currently the content treats all days equally. A generic Day 14 quote doesn't cut it when you're 10 days post-transfer and losing your mind.
Recommendation
Create a TWW-specific content track that activates based on treatment type and day. Content should: (1) Acknowledge the specific anxiety ("We know you're wondering. It's okay."), (2) Redirect from symptom-googling ("Close that browser tab. Come breathe with us instead."), (3) Provide distraction activities, not just reflection, (4) Include specific affirmations about patience and uncertainty. This is a content strategy task, not engineering — but the sub-phases system already supports it.
2.4 Miscarriage and failed cycle sensitivity  🔴 Critical
The review.html already flags this as item #1. The end-of-cycle screen needs a branch for negative outcomes. But beyond end-of-cycle, the entire app needs sensitivity review. Words like "your baby," "when you're pregnant," or overly optimistic framing can be devastating for someone who has experienced loss. This applies to quotes, affirmations, journal prompts, and friend notes.
Recommendation
(1) Audit all content for outcome-assuming language — replace "when you get pregnant" with "whatever comes next." (2) The end-of-cycle flow should branch into three paths as detailed in the UX review: good news / it didn't work / not sure yet. (3) If a user returns after a break, the welcome-back should be extra tender and never assume why they left. (4) Consider a "sensitive mode" toggle in settings that adjusts tone.
2.5 Community or peer connection  🟢 Nice-to-have
Fertility treatment is isolating. Many women feel like nobody else understands. The app currently provides content but no sense of "I'm not alone." Building a full community feature is a big lift, but even small signals of togetherness help.
Recommendation
Start small: (1) "X other women are on Day 8 with you today" — anonymous count, no interaction needed. (2) Daily anonymous poll ("How are you feeling today?" → see results → "42% of women on Day 8 also feel anxious"). (3) Curated "stories from others" quotes from real users who've been through it (submitted voluntarily). These create solidarity without the moderation burden of a full community.
2.6 Clinic appointment reminders  🟢 Nice-to-have
During treatment, clinic appointments are frequent and high-stakes (scans, blood draws, egg retrieval, transfer). The app knows which treatment type and day the user is on, so it could predict common appointment windows and offer gentle reminders or preparation tips.
Recommendation
Phase 1: Add a simple "Add to calendar" card at the appropriate day (e.g., "Most clinics do a scan around Day 8 — do you have one coming up?"). Phase 2: Allow manual appointment entry and send a morning-of message ("Your scan is today. You've got this."). This positions the app as a genuine companion, not just a content feed.
2.7 Post-cycle debrief with clinic notes  🟢 Nice-to-have
After a cycle ends, many clinics do a debrief appointment. Users often forget what was discussed or feel overwhelmed. A space to capture key takeaways from the debrief — what worked, what to change next time, notes for the next cycle — would be valuable, especially for users doing multiple cycles.
Recommendation
Add a "Cycle Notes" section to the end-of-cycle reflection. Prompt with: "Anything your clinic said that's worth remembering?" and "What would you do differently next time?" These notes carry forward and are visible when starting a new cycle, creating continuity between attempts.
Section 3 — UX Improvements

Interaction patterns, navigation, loading states, and structural improvements that would make the app feel more polished and intuitive.

3.1 "Skip for now" should be more prominent on every step  🟡 Important
On several onboarding and settings screens, the skip/dismiss option is either very small text, styled as a link, or missing entirely. Users in a hurry (or with decision fatigue) need a clear escape hatch at every step. This is especially important for music/Spotify and component selection, which aren't essential to get started.

→
Suggested — clear alternative

Recommendation
Make skip a proper button (not text link) with a minimum height of 44px. Add reassurance text: "You can always add this later in Settings." Apply this pattern to: Spotify connect, component selection, vibe selection, and dietary preferences.
3.2 Loading states need personality  🟡 Important
Current loading is a generic spinner or skeleton screen. For an app that's all about emotional connection, loading states are missed micro-moments. Users see loading every single day when opening the app — it should feel intentional, not empty.
Current — generic
Loading...
→
Suggested — warm loading

just for you...
Recommendation
Replace spinners with rotating warm messages: "Preparing something just for you...", "Choosing today's words...", "Finding your song...". Use the three-dot pulse animation instead of a spinner. This turns a wait into a moment of anticipation. Rotate messages every 2 seconds if loading takes longer.
3.3 Empty states need warmth  🟢 Nice-to-have
When a user hasn't saved any favourites, hasn't written any journal entries, or opens the Journey tab on Day 1, the empty state is either blank or a generic "Nothing here yet." These moments should encourage, not deflate.
Current empty states
Favourites: "No favourites saved."
Journal: (blank)
Journey: "No entries yet."
Warmer alternatives
Favourites: "Nothing saved yet — tap the heart on any quote or song that moves you."
Journal: "Your story starts here. Today's prompt is waiting for you."
Journey: "Day 1 is a beginning. Come back tomorrow to see your journey grow."
Recommendation
Every empty state should: (1) Acknowledge where the user is, (2) Invite them to take action, (3) Point them to where they can do it. Never just say "nothing here" — always suggest what to do next.
3.4 Back navigation is inconsistent  🟡 Important
Some screens have a "← Back" text link (top left), some use a chevron icon, some have no back button at all (e.g., onboarding steps, paywall). The browser back button works but sometimes breaks state (going back from payment to account screen loses form data). Users expect consistent, predictable navigation.
Recommendation
Standardise on a consistent back pattern: left chevron icon + "Back" text, 44px touch target, always in the same position (top-left, 16px from edges). On screens where "back" would be destructive (e.g., mid-payment), show a "Cancel" instead with a confirmation dialog. Test browser back button on every screen transition to ensure state is preserved.
3.5 Bottom nav — confirm 3-tab structure (Today, Journey, Settings)  🟢 Nice-to-have
The app should have a clear bottom navigation with three tabs: Today (daily content), Journey (timeline/progress), and Settings (preferences, account, pause). This needs verification — is it implemented, and is the active tab state clearly visible?

Recommendation
Verify the bottom nav is implemented with: (1) Clear active state (accent color for active tab), (2) Labels under icons (not icons alone), (3) 48px minimum touch target height for the entire nav bar, (4) The nav hides during onboarding and only appears after Day 1 content loads. If not yet implemented, this should be straightforward since the routing structure already exists.
Section 4 — Wording Improvements

The words in this app matter more than in most products. The audience is emotionally vulnerable. Every label, button, and message should feel like it was written by someone who understands.

4.1 "Done ✓" button could be warmer  🟢 Nice-to-have
The "Done ✓" button at the bottom of each day's content is functional but clinical. For an app about emotional support during fertility treatment, completing a day is an act of self-care worth celebrating. "Done" treats it like a checkbox on a to-do list.
Current
Done ✓
Options to consider
• "I showed up today" ✨
• "Today is complete" ✨
• "I'm here. That's enough." ✨
• Keep "Done ✓" but add a micro-celebration
Recommendation
The strongest option: "I showed up today" — it reframes completion as an achievement of presence, not task completion. It validates the user. If the team prefers keeping "Done," add a micro-celebration: confetti particles, a gentle pulse, or a brief message that appears after tapping ("You showed up. That matters.").
4.2 "Not for me" on nutrition cards  🟢 Nice-to-have
The dismiss action on nutrition/fuel cards currently says "Not for me." This wording implies rejection — the user is saying no to food the app suggested. During fertility treatment when diet feels loaded and stressful, even small wording choices around food matter. "Not for me" could trigger guilt.
Current
"Not for me" 👎
Warmer alternatives
• "Try something else" 🔄
• "Show me another" ✨
• "Swap this" 🔀
Recommendation
Change to "Try something else" or "Show me another." This frames the action as curiosity rather than rejection. The button should refresh/swap the card with an alternative suggestion rather than dismissing the nutrition component entirely.
4.3 Register Gate wording — confirm improvements  🟡 Important
The register-gate-options.html page explored 4 different approaches to the register gate wording. The key improvements were: (1) Clearly state "You've completed 3 free days" instead of vague "Ready to keep going?", (2) Separate account creation (free) from payment (paid), (3) Make the skip option honest ("Stay on my free days for now"). Which option was chosen and is it implemented?
Recommendation
Verify the current implementation matches the chosen option. Key elements that must be present: (1) The number "3" should appear — users need to know why they're being gated. (2) "Create account" should be clearly separate from "Pay." (3) The skip option must not be misleading. If Option B or D was chosen, confirm the "What you'll unlock" list is accurate and current.
4.4 End-of-cycle reflection question alternatives  🟡 Important
The end-of-cycle reflection should adapt its questions based on the user's emotional state. A one-size-fits-all "What did you learn?" can feel dismissive if the cycle failed. The reflection needs branching paths that respond to whether the outcome was positive, negative, or uncertain.
Generic (risky)
"What did you learn from this cycle?"
"What's your favourite memory?"
"How will you celebrate?"
Outcome-sensitive
Good news path:
"What moment from this cycle will you carry with you?"

Didn't work path:
"What's one thing you want to remember about your strength during this cycle?"

Uncertain path:
"What would you tell yourself on Day 1 of this cycle?"
Recommendation
Use the end-of-cycle branching (from review.html item #1) to select appropriate reflection questions. After the user indicates their outcome, serve 2-3 reflection prompts matched to their emotional state. All prompts should focus on the user's strength and presence, never on the outcome itself.
Section 5 — Visual Polish

Design consistency, accessibility, and the small details that make an app feel professionally crafted rather than a prototype.

5.1 Card visual consistency across 5 vibes  🟡 Important
Each vibe (fierce, nurturing, calm, lighthearted, spiritual) has its own colour palette and emoji set. However, the card structure (padding, border-radius, label positioning, font sizes) may vary inconsistently across vibes. A quote card in "fierce" vibe should have the same structural layout as one in "calm" — only the colours and tone should change, not the architecture.
Fierce
🔥 TODAY'S FIRE
"She was fierce."
— Author
Calm
🌊 GENTLE WAVE
"Be still and know."
— Author
Spiritual
✨ DIVINE LIGHT
"Trust the timing."
— Author
Recommendation
Audit all 5 vibe themes and ensure: (1) Same card padding (16px), border-radius (14px), and label positioning. (2) Same font sizes for quote (16-18px), label (9px mono), author (9px mono). (3) Only the accent colour, background tint, emoji, and label text change per vibe. Create a shared CSS class system rather than inline styles per vibe.
5.2 Quote font size (22px) may be too large on small screens  🟡 Important
The daily quote renders at 22px Cormorant Garamond italic. On a 375px-wide iPhone SE, a longer quote (2-3 sentences) can overflow awkwardly or require excessive scrolling. This is the first thing users see each day — if it looks cramped or broken, the magic is lost.
Large screen (390px) — fine
"She was powerful not because she wasn't scared but because she went on so strongly."
— Atticus
Small screen (320px) — cramped
"She was powerful not because she wasn't scared but because she went on so strongly."
— Atticus
Same font size, less space = worse readability
Recommendation
Use responsive font sizing: clamp(18px, 4.5vw, 24px) for quotes. This scales gracefully from small phones (18px) to tablets (24px). Also consider dynamic sizing based on quote length: shorter quotes get larger text, longer quotes get slightly smaller.
5.3 Colour contrast on calm and spiritual vibes  🟡 Important
The calm vibe uses light blues and the spiritual vibe uses soft purples. Against the dark background (#1C0F0C or #0E0E0E), these lighter accent colours may not meet WCAG AA contrast requirements (4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text). Users reading in dim light or with visual impairments may struggle.
Potential contrast issue

→

Recommendation
Run all vibe accent colours through a contrast checker (WebAIM). Replace any rgba/opacity colours with solid hex values that meet WCAG AA. For calm: use #8FB8D8 (labels) and #A3C7E0 (body). For spiritual: use #C4A0E8 (labels) and #D0B4F0 (body). Test in both light and dark modes.
5.4 Touch targets must be minimum 44px  🟡 Important
Apple's HIG and Google's Material Design both specify minimum 44x44px touch targets. Several elements in the app may be smaller: the heart/favourite icon, the share icon, "skip" text links, the back arrow, bottom nav items, and component toggle switches. During fertility treatment, users may be shaky (post-injection), emotional, or distracted — small targets are extra frustrating.

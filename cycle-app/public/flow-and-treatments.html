2. Treatment types & stages

For audit item F4 (onboarding doesn't ask about treatment stage). Here's which treatments actually have discrete clinical stages, what those stages feel like emotionally, and whether a "stage" question in onboarding would add value.

IVF — In Vitro Fertilization

Default 18 days · most common treatment · highest emotional load
Pre-cycle prep (variable)
Stimulation (~10-12 days)
Trigger shot (1 day)
Egg retrieval (day 0)
Embryo development (3-5 days)
Fresh transfer OR freeze
Two-week wait (14 days)
Beta hCG test

Stimulation feels physically heavy (bloating, injections, mood swings). Two-week wait is the most emotionally loaded stage — pure anxiety, waiting. Transfer day feels ceremonial. Each stage benefits from very different content.

Stage question adds value? YES — high. Can gate content tone: stims get grounding+body content; 2WW gets distraction+hope content; post-beta gets either celebration or grief support.

ICSI — Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection

Default 18 days · clinically same stages as IVF
Stimulation
Retrieval
Lab: ICSI injection
Transfer
Two-week wait

ICSI is a lab technique, not a different patient experience. Patient-side flow is identical to IVF.

Stage question adds value? YES — same as IVF (treat as same flow).

Egg Freezing

Default 14 days · shorter than IVF (no transfer/2WW)
Stimulation (~10-12 days)
Trigger
Egg retrieval
Recovery (3-5 days)

Emotional arc is different from IVF — no 2WW, no "is it working" loop. More about self-advocacy, autonomy, often single-person. Very different emotional need from someone mid-IVF.

Stage question adds value? MEDIUM — 2 meaningful stages (pre-retrieval vs recovery). Could just infer from day number instead (days 1-12 = stims, 13-14 = recovery).

Egg Donation

Default 14 days · recipient or donor — very different experience
Donor path: stims → retrieval
Recipient path: prep → transfer → 2WW

Without distinguishing donor vs recipient, content will miss the mark entirely. A recipient is emotionally much closer to an IVF patient (hoping for embryo success); a donor is closer to egg-freezing emotionally.

Stage question adds value? HIGH — in fact this one almost needs a "role" question (donor or recipient) before stage.

IUI — Intrauterine Insemination

Default 28 days · shorter and less physically intense, longer cycle arc
Cycle monitoring (~10-14 days)
Trigger shot
Insemination (1 day)
Two-week wait
Beta test

IUI patients are often doing this after trying naturally — there's a "if this doesn't work, IVF next" layer. Less invasive, but often more cycles.

Stage question adds value? LOW-MED — only 2 really distinct stages (pre-procedure vs 2WW). The 28-day cycle makes day-number inference more reliable.

Embryo Transfer / FET

Default 18 days · medicated prep + transfer + 2WW
Prep cycle (~14 days estrogen, lining build)
Progesterone start
Transfer day
Two-week wait

Different from fresh IVF transfer because there's no retrieval stress. Often people doing FET have already been through retrieval — grief, loss, or success from last round colours this one. Emotionally nuanced.

Stage question adds value? MEDIUM — but asking "is this your first transfer or a repeat?" would add more value than stage alone.

Medicated Cycle

Default 21 days · umbrella term for assisted natural cycle

Usually fertility meds (Clomid, Letrozole) with timed intercourse or monitoring. Early-journey experience. Lower physical load, longer emotional tail because often repeated many cycles.

Stage question adds value? LOW — too variable. Better to ask "which cycle number is this for you?" to catch repeat-cycle emotional fatigue.

Preparing

Default 14 days · pre-treatment prep (supplements, lifestyle, mental)

Unique user — not actively in a treatment cycle yet. Content should be optimistic and empowering, focused on building readiness. Very different tone from in-cycle users.

Stage question adds value? LOW — Preparing IS a stage. Already stage-tagged by treatment choice.

Surrogacy

Default 28 days · intended parent experience, not physical stages

Intended parent is not physically going through treatment — emotional stages are totally different (legal, waiting for gestational carrier, bonding-at-a-distance). Needs its own content track entirely.

Stage question adds value? LOW for surrogacy specifically — this flow may need different content altogether, not just a stage variant.

3. Recommendation

Don't add a generic "what stage are you in?" step to onboarding. It'll feel clinical and miss the nuance. Instead:

  1. IVF / ICSI: Add a targeted step only for these two — the content-tone payoff is biggest. Ask "where are you starting?" with 3 options: Before stims · Mid-cycle · Waiting (2WW). Use this to skew the first 3-5 days of content.
  2. Egg Donation: Add a role question (donor / recipient) — different enough to justify.
  3. FET: Add a "first transfer or repeat?" question. Repeat transfers need emotional acknowledgment of prior loss/wait.
  4. IUI / Egg Freezing / Medicated / Preparing / Surrogacy: No stage question — infer from day number or skip.
  5. How to keep it light: conditional — only show the stage step when the user picks IVF, ICSI, Egg Donation, or FET. Keep it to 3-option buttons, one screen, skippable.

Bottom line: 80% of the value comes from asking IVF/ICSI patients where they're starting. Everything else is low-yield.

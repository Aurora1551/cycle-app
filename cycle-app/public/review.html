Critical — Will lose users
01 No "bad day" or failed cycle path Critical
If a user's cycle fails, transfer doesn't work, or they get a negative test — the app has nothing for them. It keeps showing cheerful quotes and affirmations. On r/infertility and r/IVF, this is the #1 complaint about wellness apps. Users going through the hardest moment of their journey get tone-deaf positivity.
Suggested fix
Add a "This cycle didn't go as planned" option accessible from the day screen or settings. Lead to grief-appropriate content: acknowledgment ("This is devastating, and your feelings are valid"), a gentle breathing exercise, a journal prompt about processing loss, and an option to pause the cycle. The AI content generation should shift tone entirely when this is active.
02 No pause functionality Critical
If a user needs a break (bad news, procedure recovery, emotional burnout), they can't pause. The daily content keeps piling up, and they feel behind. This creates guilt, and guilty users churn silently rather than coming back.
Suggested fix
Add a "Take a break" option in Settings. Pauses the cycle counter. When they return, show a gentle "Welcome back" screen that acknowledges the break without asking why. Don't lose any progress or streak data.
03 Content will feel repetitive by week 2 Critical
The AI generates similar-toned content daily using the same vibe instructions. By day 8-10, quotes start sounding alike, affirmations blur together, journal prompts repeat themes. This is the "content wall" — the #1 churn driver in daily content apps.
Suggested fix
Vary content type and emotional tone across the cycle. Early days: hopeful/energetic. Mid-cycle: grounding/patient. Late cycle (TWW): calming/reassuring. Pass the cycle phase to the AI prompt. Also rotate card order day-to-day so the page layout feels fresh. Consider adding a new content type every few days (e.g., "fun fact about your body" on day 5, "letter to future you" on day 10).
High Priority — Significantly impacts experience
04 Onboarding has no skip option High
Every onboarding step requires a selection. No "Skip" or "I'll decide later." If someone gets stuck at treatment type (their situation is complex, they're not sure, or they're just exploring), they abandon. Required fields on emotional questions create friction.
Suggested fix
Add "Skip for now" as a subtle text link on steps 2-6 (keep name required). Default to reasonable fallbacks: general wellness content, calm vibe, all components enabled.
05 No onboarding resume if interrupted High
If a user closes the app mid-onboarding (phone call, notification, battery dies), they restart from step 1. On mobile, interruptions are constant. Redoing 3-4 steps after an interruption causes immediate abandonment.
Suggested fix
Save each onboarding step to localStorage as the user completes it (not just the final profile). On app open, check for partial onboarding data and resume from the last completed step.
06 Paywall has no social proof High
Users are asked to pay £9.99 with zero evidence that it works. No testimonials, no user count, no ratings, no "as featured in" badges. For a health/wellness app targeting a vulnerable audience, trust is everything. Without it, conversion stays low.
Suggested fix
Add 1-2 short testimonials below the plan cards. E.g.: "The daily quotes got me through my TWW. I actually looked forward to opening the app." — Sarah, IVF cycle 2. Even placeholder quotes initially signal that real people use this.
07 "Complete Day" action is confusing High
There's a button to mark the day done, but it's not clear what "completing" means. Do I need to read everything? Do the breathing? Just open it? Confused users either don't engage or don't feel accomplished when they do.
Suggested fix
Add a gentle "Done for today" button at the bottom of the content cards with a brief celebration micro-animation (confetti or a gentle glow). Don't require any specific action — the value is in showing up. Label it "I showed up today" rather than "Complete Day."
08 No treatment-phase awareness High
The app knows the user is doing IVF but doesn't know what phase: stims, retrieval, transfer, TWW. Content should shift dramatically between these. A quote about patience on stim day 1 is irrelevant; on TWW day 8 it's everything. Generic content undermines the personalisation promise.
Suggested fix
Add a treatment phase selector (even just: preparing, stimming, procedure week, waiting, results). This could be a new onboarding step or auto-inferred from cycle day + treatment type. Feed it to the AI content generation prompt so tone and themes shift with the phase.
Medium Priority — Improves retention
09 No streaks or gentle accountability Medium
Daily apps live or die on return visits. There's no streak counter, no "7 days in a row" moment, nothing that makes tomorrow feel different from skipping.
Suggested fix
Add a subtle streak counter in the day header card. Not aggressive — frame it as "7 days of showing up for yourself" rather than a gamified number. Celebrate milestones gently at 3, 7, 14, and cycle-end.
10 No evening check-in Medium
The app is morning-only. Fertility journeys are hardest at night — lying in bed overthinking, doom-scrolling, worrying about tomorrow's appointment. A short evening touchpoint would catch users when they need support most.
Suggested fix
Add an optional evening card: a single calming quote or 20-second breathing exercise. Could be triggered by the evening notification time already in settings. Keep it light — one card, not a full day screen.
11 No way to revisit favorite content Medium
Once a day passes, the quote, affirmation, and journal prompt are gone. Users can't bookmark the one that really resonated. The quote that got someone through a hard afternoon disappears by tomorrow.
Suggested fix
Add a "Save" heart icon on quote and affirmation cards. Store favorites in localStorage. Show them in a "Favorites" section accessible from Progress or Settings.
12 End of Cycle has no reflection Medium
The cycle ends with stats and a share button, but no emotional closure. No "What did you learn about yourself?" or "What surprised you?" The most meaningful moment of the journey is reduced to numbers.
Suggested fix
Add a short reflection journal prompt before showing the stats. Make it optional but prominent. E.g., "Before we celebrate — what's one thing you want to remember from this cycle?" Save the response and show it if they start a new cycle.
13 No partner involvement Medium
Fertility is rarely a solo journey. Partners want to help but don't know how. There's no way to include them. This limits the app's reach and the support system it could create.
Suggested fix
Light version: Add a "Share today's quote" button via WhatsApp/text on the quote card. Medium version: Add partner-specific daily prompts ("Ask your partner how they're feeling about tomorrow"). These are low-effort features with high emotional value.
14 No emotional state logging Medium
The app sends content but never asks how the user is feeling. This is a missed opportunity for personalisation (content could adapt to mood) and self-awareness (users could see mood trends over the cycle).
Suggested fix
Add a simple mood selector (5 emojis: great, good, okay, tough, hard) at the top of the day screen. Use it to subtly adjust content tone via the AI prompt. Show mood trends as a simple line in the Progress tab.
Quick Wins — Low effort, high impact
15 Splash screen has too many options Quick Win
Three equal-sized buttons (Begin, I have an account, Gift) plus a language selector. New users hesitate when given too many equal choices. The primary action doesn't stand out enough from secondary ones.
Suggested fix
Keep "Begin" as the dominant hero CTA. Make "I have an account" a text link (not a button). Move "Gift it to someone" to a smaller, separate text link. Reduce visual weight of secondary options.
16 "Take a moment" button doesn't convey value Quick Win
The breathing exercise CTA says "Take a moment · ~35s" which sounds like a task. Users skip things that sound like effort, especially when they're already exhausted from treatment.
Suggested fix
Rename to "Breathe with me · 35s" — the word "breathe" is instantly calming, and "with me" creates intimacy. Alternatively, just "Your Moment" with a play icon.
17 Add "Share this quote" to quote card Quick Win
Quotes are the most shareable content type in the app. There's no easy way to share one. Every share is free marketing to someone who might also be going through fertility treatment.
Suggested fix
Add a small share icon on the quote card. On tap, open native share sheet or copy the quote + "via Cycle app" attribution. Could also generate a branded image for Instagram stories.
18 Add medication/appointment reminder card Quick Win
The #1 feature request on fertility forums. Users juggle complex medication schedules (sometimes 3-4 injections at specific times). Even a basic reminder would make the app indispensable — it becomes the thing they open every morning.
Suggested fix
Add a "Today's reminders" card at the top of the day screen. Simple text input where users add 1-3 reminders with optional times. Stored in localStorage. No backend needed. This single feature could drive daily opens more than any content.
19 Show blurred content preview on paywall Quick Win
The paywall shows plan options but no preview of what you're unlocking. Users don't know what "One Cycle" actually contains. The locked day 4 screen does show blurred cards, but the paywall itself (shown during onboarding or from the register gate) doesn't.
Suggested fix
Add a "What you'll get" section below the plans with icons + short descriptions: daily personalised quotes, curated music, guided journaling, breathing exercises, affirmations. Make it visual, not just text. Show what a day looks like.

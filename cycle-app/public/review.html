Critical — Will lose users
01 No "bad day" or failed cycle path Critical
You only know a cycle failed at the end — when results come in. You don't know on day 8. So this isn't a mid-cycle feature, it's an end-of-cycle branch.

Currently the end-of-cycle screen is purely celebratory ("You did it!", stats, share). But not every cycle ends in good news. If a user just got a negative result, the celebration screen is devastating. It needs a second path.

How it differs from Pause (#2): Pause = "I need a break." This = "My cycle ended and it didn't work." Pause happens anytime. This happens specifically at the end.
Suggested fix
Restructure the end-of-cycle screen into two moments:

Moment 1 — Celebrate the completion (everyone sees this):
"You completed this cycle." Glowing orb, stats (days completed, journal entries, songs played). This is the achievement — showing up every single day IS the thing worth celebrating, regardless of what comes next. Breathing exercise. Reflection prompt ("What's one thing you want to remember from this cycle?").

Moment 2 — Gently ask what's next:
After the celebration, a simple question: "What's next for you?"
• "Good news — I'm moving forward" → extra celebration, option to start new cycle or gift to a friend
• "It didn't go as I hoped" → tender acknowledgment ("This is hard. You showed up every single day, and that matters."), option to take a break or start a new cycle when ready
• "I'm not sure yet" → gentle pause state, "We're here when you're ready"

The key: completing the cycle is always honoured first. The outcome question comes second.
02 No pause functionality Critical · IMPLEMENTED
Already built. "Take a break" toggle in Settings pauses the cycle counter. Welcome-back overlay on return says "You took a break, and that's okay." No progress lost.

Different from #1: Pause is for when you just need to step away — between cycles, recovering from procedure, emotional exhaustion. No bad news necessarily, just need space. #1 is specifically about the cycle failing and needing grief-appropriate content.
Status
Done. Available in Settings → "Take a break" row. Freezes cycle counter, shows gentle welcome-back on return.
03 Content will feel repetitive by week 2 Critical · PARTIALLY DONE
The AI generates similar-toned content daily. By day 8-10, quotes start sounding alike, affirmations blur together. This is the "content wall" — the #1 churn driver in daily content apps.
What's done
Cycle-phase tone variation is implemented. The AI prompt now shifts emotional tone across 5 phases: beginning (hopeful), early (building momentum), middle (grounding/patient), late (calming/reassuring), final (celebratory but tender). This is in server.js via getCyclePhase().

Card order stays fixed — you rightly flagged that rotating cards would feel inconsistent and disruptive. The layout is the same every day (quote → music → affirmation → journal → breathing → friend note). Consistency in structure, variation in substance.

What's not done yet: Varying content depth within each phase — e.g., early journal prompts are simple ("What are you carrying into this cycle?") while mid-cycle prompts go deeper ("What have you learned about patience?"). This would need more specific instructions in the AI prompt per phase.
High Priority — Significantly impacts experience
04 Onboarding has no skip option High · IMPLEMENTED
Every onboarding step required a selection. Users stuck at treatment type (complex situation, exploring) would abandon.
Status
Done. "Skip for now" link added on steps 2-6 (name stays required). Defaults: treatment=IVF, cycle=28 days, components=all main ones, vibe=calm, music=pop+ambient. Users can change everything later in Settings.

Open question: Should the skip default use "fertility journey" instead of "IVF" to avoid showing the wrong treatment? Would need a neutral AI prompt path. Currently defaults to IVF since it's most common.
05 No onboarding resume if interrupted High · IMPLEMENTED
If a user closed the app mid-onboarding, they'd restart from step 1.
Status
Done. Each onboarding step saves to localStorage via the existing update() function. On app reopen, if partial profile data exists (name but missing later fields), the app resumes from the exact step where they left off. Subtle — no "resume?" dialog, just picks up where they were.
06 Paywall has no social proof High
Users are asked to pay £9.99 with zero evidence that the app works. No testimonials, no user count. Trust is everything for a health/wellness app targeting a vulnerable audience.
Suggested fix
Add a short testimonial below the plan cards on the paywall. Something like:

"I just wanted something that showed up for me every morning. Cycle was that."
— W., egg freezing

Add a second testimonial later from a beta tester or early user.
07 "Complete Day" action is confusing High
There's a button to mark the day done, but it's not clear what "completing" means. Do I need to read everything? Do the breathing? Just open it? Confused users either don't engage or don't feel accomplished when they do.
Suggested fix
Add a gentle "Done for today" button at the bottom of the content cards with a brief celebration micro-animation (confetti or a gentle glow). Don't require any specific action — the value is in showing up. Label it "I showed up today" rather than "Complete Day."
08 No treatment-phase awareness High
The app knows the user is doing IVF but not what phase: stims, retrieval, transfer, TWW. Protocols vary hugely user to user — some stim for 8 days, others 14. Fresh transfer vs freeze-all changes everything.
Revised approach
Deferred to v2. The cycle-phase tone variation already implemented (beginning/early/middle/late/final based on day number) gives 80% of the benefit without asking the user anything. Adding treatment-phase questions would clutter the experience and ask too much of the user. Revisit once there's user feedback requesting it.
Medium Priority — Improves retention
09 No streaks or gentle accountability Medium
Daily apps live or die on return visits. There's no streak counter, no "7 days in a row" moment, nothing that makes tomorrow feel different from skipping.
Suggested fix
Add a subtle streak counter in the day header card. Not aggressive — frame it as "7 days of showing up for yourself" rather than a gamified number. Celebrate milestones gently at 3, 7, 14, and cycle-end.
10 No evening check-in Medium
The app generates one content set per day. If you open at 10pm, you see the same cards as 7am. There's no evening-specific content and no evening notification to trigger a second visit.
How it would work
The app checks the local time when opened. If after ~7pm, it shows an additional "wind down" card at the top — a calming quote or short breathing exercise, different from the morning content. No separate notification needed — it just adapts to when the user opens it.

Complexity: Requires generating two content sets per day (morning + evening) in one API call, time detection, and handling edge cases (what if someone only opens at night — do they miss morning content? Answer: no, they see both, evening card just appears at the top).

Recommendation: v2. Adds real value but needs design work. For launch, the single daily content set works fine. Most users will open in the morning via notification.
11 No way to revisit favorite content Medium
Once a day passes, the quote, affirmation, and journal prompt are gone. Users can't bookmark the one that really resonated.
Suggested fix
Add a "Save" heart icon on quote and affirmation cards. Store favorites in localStorage (key: cycle_favorites). Show saved items in a small "Saved" section within the Progress tab — not a separate page, just a collapsible section below the day grid. No analytics page needed.
12 End of Cycle has no reflection Medium
The cycle ends with stats and a share button, but no emotional closure. No "What did you learn about yourself?" or "What surprised you?" The most meaningful moment of the journey is reduced to numbers.
Suggested fix
Add a short reflection journal prompt before showing the stats. Make it optional but prominent. E.g., "Before we celebrate — what's one thing you want to remember from this cycle?" Save the response and show it if they start a new cycle.
13 Share today's quote Medium
Quotes are the most shareable content in the app. There's no way to share one. Every share is free marketing to someone who may also be going through fertility treatment.
Revised approach — Share only, no partner prompts
Dropped the partner-specific prompts idea. The woman is already doing the injections, the appointments, the emotional labor. Adding "tell your partner to do this" puts MORE work on her.

Instead: add a share icon on the quote card. One tap → share sheet or WhatsApp.

No approvals needed. Two approaches, both zero-backend:
• Web Share API (navigator.share) — triggers the native OS share sheet on mobile. WhatsApp, iMessage, Instagram etc. appear automatically. Works on iOS Safari 15+ and Android Chrome.
• wa.me fallback — https://wa.me/?text=ENCODED_TEXT opens WhatsApp directly with pre-filled text. No API key, no WhatsApp Business account, no approval.

The share text would be: "[quote]" — [author] · Shared via Cycle + app link. No backend changes, no API keys, no approvals of any kind. Ready to implement.
14 No emotional state logging Medium
The app sends content but never asks how the user is feeling. This could help personalise content tone (AI adapts to mood) and build self-awareness.
Revised approach — Mood logging only, no trend charts
Add a simple mood selector (5 emojis) at the top of the day screen. Use it to subtly adjust content tone via the AI prompt (e.g., if user selects "tough", shift quotes toward acknowledging difficulty rather than cheerful motivation).

Dropped the mood trends/progress chart idea. Showing mood data on the Progress tab could feel clinical or discouraging if someone sees a lot of "hard" days. The mood selector should feel like a gentle check-in, not data collection. The value is in the AI adapting, not in the user analysing their own mood graph.
Quick Wins — Low effort, high impact
15 Splash screen has too many options Quick Win
Three equal-sized buttons (Begin, I have an account, Gift) plus a language selector. New users hesitate when given too many equal choices.
Suggested fix
Keep "Begin" as the dominant hero CTA. Make "I have an account" and "Gift it to someone" smaller text links (not full buttons). Reduce visual weight of secondary options so the eye goes straight to "Begin."

Note on "Skip for now" destinations: If a user skips all onboarding, they get a generic journey: IVF treatment, calm vibe, 28-day cycle, pop/ambient music, all components. They can personalise everything later in Settings. Open question: should the treatment default be "fertility journey" (generic) rather than "IVF" to avoid showing the wrong label?
16 "Take a moment" button doesn't convey value Quick Win
The breathing exercise CTA says "Take a moment · ~35s" which sounds like a task. Users skip things that sound like effort, especially when they're already exhausted from treatment.
Suggested fix
Rename to "Breathe with me · 35s" — the word "breathe" is instantly calming, and "with me" creates intimacy. Alternatively, just "Your Moment" with a play icon.
17 Add "Share this quote" to quote card Quick Win
Quotes are the most shareable content type in the app. There's no easy way to share one. Every share is free marketing to someone who might also be going through fertility treatment.
Suggested fix
Add a small share icon on the quote card. On tap, open native share sheet or copy the quote + "via Cycle app" attribution. Could also generate a branded image for Instagram stories.
18 Add medication/appointment reminder card Quick Win
The #1 feature request on fertility forums. Users juggle complex medication schedules (sometimes 3-4 injections at specific times). Even a basic reminder would make the app indispensable — it becomes the thing they open every morning.
Suggested fix
Add a "Today's reminders" card at the top of the day screen. Simple text input where users add 1-3 reminders with optional times. Stored in localStorage. No backend needed. This single feature could drive daily opens more than any content.
19 Show blurred content preview on paywall Quick Win
The paywall shows plan options but no preview of what you're unlocking. Users don't know what "One Cycle" actually contains. The locked day 4 screen does show blurred cards, but the paywall itself (shown during onboarding or from the register gate) doesn't.
Suggested fix
Add a "What you'll get" section below the plans with icons + short descriptions: daily personalised quotes, curated music, guided journaling, breathing exercises, affirmations. Make it visual, not just text. Show what a day looks like.

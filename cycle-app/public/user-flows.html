What the buyer fills in

FOR THEM — who the gift is for
  • Their first name — used to personalise the email greeting ("Hello Aurora,") and shown on the Confirmation screen
  • Their email — where the redeem link is delivered
  • Your message (optional) — quoted in the recipient email, limited to 500 chars
FROM YOU — who the gift is from
  • Your first name — used as the "from" in the recipient email subject and body. Without it the email reads "kate@example.com gifted you a Cycle". With it, "Kate gifted you a Cycle 🎁".
  • Your email — passed to Stripe as receipt_email (Stripe auto-sends a tax-compliant receipt), and used by us to send the branded Cycle receipt via Resend.
Buyer is not logged in: all four FROM YOU + FOR THEM fields are empty and must be typed.
Buyer is logged in: Your first name and Your email prefill from their Cycle account. They can still edit.
Continue to payment is disabled unless Their email AND Your email are both filled. Name + message are optional (we fall back to email when name is missing).

What the server does:
  1. POST /api/gift/create-intent — creates Stripe PaymentIntent, returns client secret
  2. Client confirms payment inline with Stripe Elements
  3. POST /api/gift/confirm — generates gift code, writes to gifts table, sends both emails
  4. Recipient clicks link → GET /api/gift/lookup/:code validates
  5. Recipient creates account → POST /api/register + POST /api/gift/redeem marks it consumed (once only)

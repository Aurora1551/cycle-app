1. Flow issues

HIGHNEEDS REVIEW
F1Paywall gate at Day 4 is abrupt
User completes 3 days, hits lock with 2 blurred cards. No free preview of Day 4's flavour, no trial period, no "keep the streak" emotional hook. In a vulnerable state, a hard wall can read as transactional.
Suggestion: Offer 7-day free trial with one-tap cancel, OR give Day 4 in full with gentle "you've unlocked this — continue your journey with X" prompt at Day 5. Competitor Expectful gives 7 days; Flo gives 7-day premium trial.
Review needed: which model fits your business plan?
HIGH
F2No cross-device sync before account creation
User can run the entire onboarding + 3 days of content as a guest. If they switch devices or clear cookies, all emotional state is gone. For a treatment companion where streaks matter emotionally, this is high-risk.
Suggestion: Prompt for lightweight account (email or magic link) at end of onboarding, before Day 1 content. Frame as "Save your journey" not "Sign up."
HIGHNEEDS REVIEW
F3Log out = full reset with no warning
Settings → Log out resets all localStorage and returns to splash. Loses journal entries, favourites, streak. Expected behaviour for "log out" is to preserve server state. Users may log out to switch accounts and lose everything.
Suggestion: Either (a) rename button "Start over" or "Reset journey" with confirm dialog, OR (b) actual logout that preserves Supabase-stored data across sessions.
Review needed: what's the intended semantic?
MED
F4Onboarding doesn't ask about treatment stage
User picks treatment type (IVF, IUI, etc.) and cycle length — but no question about where they are in the journey (pre-transfer, stims, 2WW, post-beta). The content is the same for Day 1 whether it's injection day 1 or embryo transfer day.
Suggestion: Add one optional step: "where are you in your cycle?" with 3-4 options. Use this to subtly tune tone of Day 1 opening without forking all content.
MED
F5No visible re-entry path to onboarding
If user wants to change vibe or treatment type later, Settings has overlays but finding them is discovery-only. No "edit my profile" top-level affordance.
Suggestion: In Settings, group under a visible "Personalisation" section header with clear sub-items (Vibe, Components, Genres, Treatment).
MED
F6End-of-cycle screen has no clear next action
After the glowing orb moment, what's next? Does the user restart? Continue to a new cycle? See a summary? Unclear flow state.
Suggestion: Add a narrative summary ("Your cycle in words") + clear CTAs: Start a new cycle / Share milestone / Go to journal archive.

2. Language & copy

QUICK WIN
L1"From your person" — ambiguous (addressed)
Updated to "A note for you" this session. Less assumption-laden, works for single/partnered/solo users.
HIGHNEEDS REVIEW
L2App name "Cycle" collides with menstrual tracking
Target market will first associate "Cycle" with period tracking (Flo, Clue, Natural Cycles). Fertility treatment is its own emotional category — the name may make users bounce when searching.
Suggestion: Consider a name that signals companionship/treatment: e.g., "Held", "Warmth", "The Daily" (just examples). Review needed: huge decision, flagging as worth discussing.
MEDNEEDS REVIEW
L3Vibe names — "Fierce" / "Warrior" framing
For a user mid-treatment who feels depleted, being called a "warrior" or expected to be "fierce" can feel demanding rather than supportive. Research suggests fertility patients often reject combat metaphors.
Option to review:
Current
Fierce 🔥 "She's Got This"
"bold, fierce, warrior energy"
Alt
Strong 🔥 "She's Strong"
"grounded strength, steady resolve"
Keeps the energy vibe option but reframes away from combat.
MED
L4Medical disclaimer only on food section
"For inspiration only · follow your clinic's guidance" appears on fuel. But affirmations, quotes, breathing content — all lack this. Important for reg/compliance and user trust.
Suggestion: One-time modal on first open: "Cycle is for emotional support, not medical advice. Always consult your clinic." Plus footer link in Settings → About.
MEDNEEDS REVIEW
L5Day header phrasing "She's At Peace / She's Got This"
Third-person framing ("She") feels slightly detached. First-person ("I am at peace" / "I've got this") is more affirmation-psychology correct, but changes the voice.
Review needed: Do you want first-person (empowering, self-talk) or third-person (observer tone, gentler)?
LOW

"favorites" (US), "yoghurt" (UK) co-exist. Pick one.
Suggestion: Given the £9.99 pricing, align on UK English throughout.

3. Layout & UI

HIGH
U1Heavy emoji density in Day screen
Every card has an emoji prefix, section labels include emojis, bottom nav has mixed icons+emoji. For users who want gravitas, this can feel juvenile. Flo/Clue use refined line-icon sets for this reason.
Suggestion: Replace functional emojis (card labels, nav) with a consistent line-icon set (Feather/Lucide). Keep emotive emojis (streak 🌱, friend-note 💛) for decorative moments only.
MED
U2Day box circles don't scale past ~14 days
Grid shows all cycleDays as circles. At 21+ days, the row wraps and circles shrink below touch-target minimum. Also no weeks grouping on the main Day header — unlike Journey tab which groups by week.
Suggestion: Cap Day header circle row to 7 (current week), let full grid live on Journey. Add week label ("Week 2 of 4").
MED
U3Settings page is one long scroll
Plan, Personalisation, Notifications, Privacy, Account, Delete, Log out — all stacked. No sectioning visual hierarchy beyond section labels.
Suggestion: Break into tabs or expand/collapse sections. Or move "Danger zone" (delete, reset) to a dedicated sub-page.
QUICK WIN
U4Breathing session — "tap to end" ambiguity
During the breathing circle, small "tap to end" text is the only affordance. Users may not see it while focusing on the breath.
Suggestion: Add a tiny × close button in the top-right of the overlay that's always visible but muted.
LOW
U5Inline styles everywhere
Theming + future design-token migration will be painful. Not user-facing but will cost velocity as app grows.
Suggestion: Gradually migrate recurring patterns to CSS classes or a tiny styled-primitives layer. No rush — only when touching files.

4. Market comparison

Direct competitors in fertility-support space

  • Expectful — meditation & mindfulness for fertility + pregnancy. Strong: audio-led content, expert contributors. Weak: subscription fatigue, broad (fertility through postpartum).
  • Kindbody — clinic-integrated app. Strong: medical integration, appointment sync. Weak: only works if you're at their clinic.
  • FertilityIQ — community + clinic reviews. Strong: peer wisdom. Weak: not a daily companion.
  • Glow/Eve — tracking-first with community. Strong: data-rich. Weak: data-pressure can worsen anxiety.
  • Proov Insight — hormone tracking. Strong: data. Weak: clinical, not emotional.
HIGH
M1Cycle's differentiator is emotional-first, not data-first
This is a real gap in the market. But the positioning needs to lean in harder. Currently the app looks like "another cycle app" on first glance — same color palette cues, similar nav.
Suggestion: Strengthen the "emotional companion" identity in app store listing, splash screen, and onboarding. Consider tagline like "You can't track your feelings. But you can be held."
MEDNEEDS REVIEW
M2Missing: partner/support-person mode
Several competitors (Kindara, Glow) have a "partner" view. For fertility, the non-carrying partner often feels helpless. A read-only partner link that shows "how she's doing this week" could be a differentiator — and justify price.
Review needed: worth a future sprint?
MED
M3No medication/injection reminders
IVF patients often juggle 4-6 injections per day with tight timing. No reminder system. Other apps (Ivy, Embie) have this as a core feature.
Suggestion: Optional injection schedule with notifications — even basic version would be a moat. Don't need to get clinical (just "time to inject" reminder).
LOW
M4No community / peer feature
Most apps in space have forums. Cycle doesn't — which can be a strength (no toxicity, no comparison) or a gap.
Suggestion: Keep community out of v1. Could add "anonymous wall" of affirmations from other users later, but it's a big moderation lift.

5. Alignment with target user

HIGH
A1User is already emotionally exhausted — minimize cognitive load
Fertility patients are doing 20+ decisions a day (which clinic, which protocol, what to eat). App adds more decisions: pick vibe, pick components, pick music, pick notifications, pick food. This can feel like work.
Suggestion: (a) Add a "just decide for me" button at each onboarding step (smart default), (b) reduce component toggle to 3 essentials with "more" expandable, (c) default notifications ON with one-tap disable.
MED
A2Premium/free boundary feels extractive in context
"You did 3 days → pay to keep going" matches the app model but not the emotional covenant. User is vulnerable; this can feel like a bait-and-switch.
Suggestion: Consider a "pay what you want" or "£9.99 one cycle, or gift it to someone going through this" framing. The current gift flow is a great start.
MEDNEEDS REVIEW
A3"Daily" expectation adds pressure on hard days
On an injection-failure day or a bad-news call, the app may feel like another thing to perform. Streaks especially can backfire ("I broke my streak on the day I found out").
Option to review: Add a "pause day" affordance that doesn't break streak. "Today is hard. I'll come back tomorrow." Button grants grace without guilt. Competitor Finch does this for mental-health tracking — users love it.
